About

Our Pre-School aims:

  • to provide a safe and stimulating learning environment where young children can develop their personal, social, emotional, physical, communication, language, mathematical and creative skills through carefully selected and enjoyable activities, planned for each child. Also, to promote an understanding of the world we live in.
  • to create a Polish-speaking community atmosphere with a caring and sharing attitude. We are happy to take any child, provided the parents are aware of the emphasis we place on the Polish language.
  • to maintain a Christian religious perspective, while respecting every child’s needs, regardless of age, gender, cultural background, race, religion or physical/mental ability. We are ready to provide professional help for children with special needs.
  • to enable young children to make the transition to a new learning environment as well prepared as possible, by providing good foundations for further learning.

For more information, please see the brochure.

The Session


09.45 - 11.20
Free play, during which the children are divided into small groups to undertake creative activities, which fit in with the day’s planning (parents are welcome to see our daily planning) and adult led activity.

11.20 - 11.25
Tidy-up time!

11.25 - 11.35
Story time, discussion, music making and prayer in the church, including singing and sharing in the group environment. The children are asked whether they want to dedicate their prayers for any particular intention.

11.40 - 11.50
Snack time. Children are encouraged to eat fruit, drink fruit juice or milk, they can also have 2 plain biscuits. The children are encouraged to take it in turns to distribute the biscuits and fruit to other children and pour their drink themselves.

11.55 - 12.05
Story time – a story is chosen to fit the day’s theme or each child can choose their own book to be read in a small group.

12.10 - 12.45
Outdoor play involving water play, sand play, activities/games with balls and hoops and riding on tricycles, or music and movement in the main hall to continue with the day’s theme, if the weather does not permit outdoor play.

Curriculum

We cover a wide range of topics each term, based around the seasons, including creative activities, which are designed to develop the children’s imaginative, social, physical, language and mathematical skills, in both Polish and English. We use a wide range of artistic media.

In keeping with the Catholic calendar, we observe Saints’ days and other Holy Days of Obligation and often invite the parents/carers to attend mass with the children. Important events in other faiths are also explored and celebrated, for example, Chinese New Year, Diwali, etc.

At the request of the parents, special attention is given to Polish customs, songs and stories.

A strong emphasis is placed on “loving your neighbour” and with this attitude we explore the multi-cultural community we live in.
